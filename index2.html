Welcome to the Lingering Garden

The Lingering Garden (留园, Liú Yuán) is one of the most famous classical gardens in China, located in Suzhou, Jiangsu Province. It was originally built in 1593 during the Ming Dynasty by a government official named Xu Taishi and later underwent several renovations and ownership changes. The garden is renowned for its exquisite layout, intricate design, and the harmonious blending of natural and artificial elements.

Covering an area of about 23,300 square meters, the Lingering Garden is divided into four distinct sections, each with its own unique style and features, including pavilions, halls, rockeries, ponds, and various plants. The garden is particularly famous for its elegant limestone formations, tranquil water features, and the use of borrowed scenery, where views from outside the garden are incorporated into the design.

A hallmark of classical Chinese garden design, the Lingering Garden emphasizes balance, harmony, and the idealization of nature, making it a UNESCO World Heritage site and a popular tourist destination for those interested in traditional Chinese culture and landscape architecture.
